UNO: Ultra Spectrum

A fork of the UNO: Full Spectrum variant

1. Overview

UNO: Ultra Spectrum builds upon the Full Spectrum version by combining multiple UNO decks and expansion packs. With added colors, new rules, and special cards, this chaotic variant takes the classic game to an entirely new level.

2. Required Decks and Cards

  • Standard UNO colors: Red, Blue, Green, Yellow
  • Extra spectrum: Orange, Purple (Neo-Partisan), Pink (Susan G. Komen), Teal (UNO Flip Express)
  • Expansion cards: Swap Pack, Reverse Pack, Show No Mercy (+6 and +10)

3. Starting the Game

  • Deal 10 cards to each player instead of 7 because of the many colors ("too many Kullas").
  • Place one card from the draw pile face-up to start the discard pile. The game begins from there.

4. Colors in Play

The official colors in the Ultra Spectrum deck are:

Red Orange Yellow Green Teal Blue Purple Pink

5. Basic Rules (UNO Refresher)

  • Players take turns matching the top card of the discard pile by either color or number.
  • If a player cannot play, they must draw one card. If they can play it, they may do so immediately.
  • Action cards (Reverse, Skip, Draw Two, Wild, etc.) behave normally unless overridden by custom rules.
  • Don’t forget to shout “UNO!” when you have one card left!

6. Extra Cards & Packs Used

  • Swap Hands Pack: Includes cards that let players swap their hands mid-game.
  • Reverse Pack: Includes Reverse +2 and other variants.
  • UNO Show No Mercy (Select Cards Only): +6 and +10 cards are included.

7. Flip Rules

This game uses the inverted side from UNO Flip Express as the “Dark Side.” When a Flip card is played, all players flip their hands and the pile. All teal cards are only usable on this side.

8. Custom Rules

Color Hue Flow

You may play a card that is next to the previous color on the hue spectrum — either forward or backward:

  • Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Teal → Blue → Purple → Pink → (back to Red)

This means you may play a color-adjacent card instead of a perfect color or number match.

Teal Card Restriction (Two-Life System)

Teal cards may only be played when the game is on the Dark Side. Playing one on the Light Side breaks the rule. Each player has two “lives” for this:

  • First mistake: Draw half a card (i.e., no penalty, just a warning).
  • Second mistake: Draw half a card (i.e., draw one card).
  • After the second mistake, both lives reset and the next violation starts the cycle again.

9. Winning

The first player to get rid of all their cards wins the round. Score based on other players’ remaining cards can be tracked optionally.
