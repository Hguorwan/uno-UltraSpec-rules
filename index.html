UNO: Ultra Spectrum

A fork of the UNO: Full Spectrum variant

1. Overview

UNO: Ultra Spectrum builds upon the Full Spectrum version by combining multiple UNO decks and adding powerful new cards and mechanics from several expansion packs. This creates a colorful and chaotic gameplay experience with more strategy and unpredictability.

2. Required Decks and Cards

  • Regular UNO (red, blue, green, yellow)
  • UNO Flip Express (teal, pink)
  • UNO Neo-Partisan (orange, purple)
  • Susan G. Komen UNO (pink)
  • Add-on packs: Reverse Pack, Swap Pack, Show No Mercy (+6 and +10 cards)

3. Colors in Play

The following colors are used in play:

Red Orange Yellow Green Teal Blue Purple Pink

4. Basic UNO Rules Refresher

  • Each player is dealt 7 cards.
  • On your turn, play one card matching either the color or number of the discard pile’s top card.
  • If you cannot play, draw one card from the draw pile.
  • If the drawn card can be played, you may play it immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.
  • Play proceeds clockwise unless reversed.
  • Special cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four) act as in regular UNO.
  • When you have one card left, say "UNO" before your next turn begins. If caught not saying it, draw two cards.
  • The first player to play all their cards wins the round.

5. Special Cards from Add-On Packs

  • Reverse +2 (Reverse Pack): Reverses play order and forces the next player to draw two cards.
  • Swap Hands (Swap Pack): All players pass their entire hand to the next player in the current direction of play.
  • +6 Card (Show No Mercy): Forces the next player to draw six cards unless they can block or stack.
  • +10 Card (Show No Mercy): Forces the next player to draw ten cards unless they can block or stack.

6. Flip Express Rules

  • When a Flip card is played, players switch decks accordingly.
  • Players may only play cards that match the color or number of the discard pile’s top card.
  • Play continues until the next Flip card reverses the side again.

7. New Custom Rules

Color Hue Direction

You may play cards in either forward or backward hue order. The color sequence is:

Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Teal → Blue → Purple → Pink → Red (repeats)

You may play a card matching either the next or previous color in this hue sequence instead of the same color.

Stacking and Blocking

  • Draw cards of the same type may be stacked, passing the cumulative draw penalty to the next player.
  • A +6 or +10 card may be blocked by playing a Wild card or a Reverse +2 card, which reverses play direction and passes the penalty on.

Color Lockdown

If the same color is played three turns in a row, that color becomes locked for one full round. No player may play that color until a Wild card is played.

Spectrum Flush Bonus

If a player manages to play at least one card of every color in the spectrum during a single round, all opponents must draw two cards.

8. Winning the Game

The game is played in rounds. The first player to reach 500 points wins. Points are scored based on cards remaining in opponents’ hands:

  • Number cards: face value
  • Draw Two, Reverse, Skip: 20 points each
  • Wild and Wild Draw Four: 50 points each
  • +6 and +10 cards: 50 points each
