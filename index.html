UNO: Ultra Spectrum

A fork of the UNO: Full Spectrum variant

1. Overview

UNO: Ultra Spectrum builds on the Full Spectrum fork by combining multiple decks and extra rules into one massive spectrum of Kullas (colors). It includes many expansions and custom mechanics for a wild experience.

2. Required Cards

  • Standard UNO: Red, Yellow, Green, Blue
  • Full Spectrum: Orange, Purple (Neo), Pink (Susan), Teal (UNO Flip)
  • Extra packs: Swap Hands, Reverse Pack, Show No Mercy (+6, +10)

3. Starting the Game

  • Each player is dealt 10 cards instead of 7 because of the many Kullas.
  • One card from the draw pile starts the game. Match by color or number.

4. All Kullas (Colors)

The playable Kullas in Ultra Spectrum UNO:

Red Orange Yellow Green Teal Blue Purple Pink

5. Standard UNO Rules

  • Match the top card by color or number.
  • If you can't play, draw one card. If it's playable, you may play it right away.
  • First to get rid of all cards wins. Don't forget to yell "UNO!" when you have one left.

6. Extra Packs Used

  • Swap Hands Pack — trade hands with another player when played.
  • Reverse Pack — includes Reverse+2 and alternate Reverse rules.
  • Show No Mercy — includes +6 and +10 cards from that set.

7. Flip Mode (Dark Side)

When a Flip card is played, everyone must flip their hand and use the "Dark Side" of the cards. The Teal Kulla is only playable while in Dark Side mode.

8. Custom Rules

  • Hue Chain Rule: You may go forward or backward in hue:
    • Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Teal → Blue → Purple → Pink → Red (looping)
    If you're on Orange, you can play Red or Yellow even if they don't match number.
  • Teal Kulla Penalty System (2 lives):
    • If a player plays a teal card while not in Dark Side mode, it's a mistake.
    • First mistake: draw half a card (i.e., nothing).
    • Second mistake: draw half a card again (i.e., draw 1 card).
    • After two mistakes, lives reset. It loops each time.

9. Winning

First player to play all their cards wins. Optionally, calculate points based on what remains in others’ hands.
